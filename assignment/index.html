Positives and Negatives of food

Positive: Local Food

Shorter distance of transportation means less greenhouse gas emissions. According to a study in 2008, about 11% of food's greenhouse gas emissions come from transportation. Columbia - How Green is Local Food

Information from another article suggests that food accounts for around 25% to 30% of global emissions.

Malik Yakini, the co-manager of D-Town farm (Detroit) says that food travels for 15 000 miles on average in the US, which makes food transportation a factor we must consider in order to reduce greenhouse gas emissions. Tomorrow

Positive: Farmer's Market

Buying from farmer's market stimulates the economy.

While many large enterprises transfer the profit to other places of the world, farmer's markets help keep the prosperity locally. It also helps with local economy: "for every $100 spent at a small business, $68 is circumvented back into the local economy" Locate Local

Moreover, it helps with national economy: "Growers selling locally create 13 full time jobs per $1 million in revenue earned. Those that do not sell locally create 3." Farmers Market Coalition

Positive: Healthy Diet

Documentary Tomorrow reveals that humans evolved to have a diet mainly consisting of perennial plants like certain fruits and vegetables. Nowadays about 60% of our diet is made up of grains, which our organism isn't designed for. According to Eat This, Not That, a grain-based diet may increase risk of cardiovascular disease and lead to nutritional deficiency.

However, In this local & zero waste meal, the majority of food are vegetables, making it healthy and suitable for our body.

Negative: Lack of GMO (Genetically Modified Organism)

GMO is good for the environment as many GM plants have more yield and less need for pesticide compared to non-GM ones.

"Over the last 25 years, GMOs have reduced pesticide applications by 7.2% and helped increase crop yields by 22%."

Thanks to crop biotechnology, many crops gained massive increase in yield between 1996 and 2020. For example, the yield of soybeans increased by 363.76 million tons and that of corn increased by 655.87 million tons. Without the presence of GMO, the same needs to be achieved with 57.8 million more acres of land.
